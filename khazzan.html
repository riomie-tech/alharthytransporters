Industrial Case Study

Khazzan Gas Field Expansion

A Precision Heavy-Lift Operation in the Heart of the Omani Desert. Delivering the backbone of national energy infrastructure under extreme conditions.

Client BP
Location Oman
Duration 6 Months
Total Tonnage 12,000 Tons
The Challenge

Engineering Against the Elements

The Khazzan Gas Field expansion required the transportation of critical 800-ton turbine units from the Port of Duqm to the inland site. This presented a logistical mountain: over 400km of unpaved desert roads and treacherous terrain.

With ambient temperatures regularly exceeding 50°C, the thermal stress on tires, hydraulic systems, and operators was immense. Every millimeter of clearance and every ton of axle load had to be calculated to prevent catastrophic road failure or equipment damage in the soft sand.

The Solution

A Systematic Approach to Complexity

Our engineering and logistics teams developed a three-phase execution plan to mitigate risk and ensure delivery.

Route Reconnaissance

30 days of intensive terrain mapping including sonar bridge testing and ground pressure simulations. We reinforced two major temporary bypasses.

Specialized Fleet

Deployment of 48-axle Goldhofer modular trailers paired with triple-headed heavy-duty prime movers for precise weight distribution.

Precision Execution

24/7 real-time telemetry monitoring from our operations center, supported by specialized escort teams and mobile mechanical workshops.
